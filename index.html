Why Water is important ?

01

Essential for Biological Processes


  • Cellular Function: At a cellular level, water is a solvent in which biochemical reactions take place. It facilitates essential processes like nutrient transportation, waste removal, and metabolic reactions within cells.

  • Homeostasis: Water helps maintain body temperature by acting as a coolant and distributor of heat. It also plays a pivotal role in maintaining the internal pH and salt balance of living organisms.




02

Supports Ecosystems and Habitats:


  • Aquatic Life: Oceans, rivers, lakes, and other water bodies support a vast array of life, from tiny plankton to massive whales. These ecosystems are entirely dependent on water for their survival.

  • Land Habitats: Terrestrial ecosystems, such as forests, jungles, and grasslands, rely on water (in the form of rainfall, rivers, etc.) to support plant and animal life. Wetlands, for instance, serve as biodiversity hotspots and act as natural water filters and flood controls.


03

Foundation of Human Civilization and Economy:


  • Agriculture: Water is fundamental for farming. It's used for irrigation to grow crops that feed billions of people and rear livestock.

  • Industry: Many industries, from manufacturing to power generation, depend on vast amounts of water for their processes.

  • Drinking and Sanitation: Clean water is indispensable for drinking, cooking, and maintaining hygiene. Access to clean water directly impacts public health and well-being.

Our Oceans

  • Pacific Ocean
  • Atlantic Ocean
  • Indian Ocean
  • Southern (or Antarctic) Ocean
  • Arctic Ocean

Pacific Ocean

The largest and deepest ocean, it covers more area than all of the Earth's landmass combined.

It's divided into the North Pacific and South Pacific by the equator.

Atlantic Ocean

The second-largest ocean, it's also split into the North Atlantic and South Atlantic by the equator.

The Atlantic Ocean is particularly notable for the role it has played in human history, especially in exploration, trade, and cultural exchange.

Indian Ocean

Located mainly in the Southern Hemisphere, it's the third-largest ocean.

It's bounded by Asia to the north, Africa to the west, and Australia to the east.

Southern (or Antarctic) Ocean

This ocean surrounds Antarctica and was officially designated as an ocean by the International Hydrographic Organization (IHO) in 2000.

It's characterized by its cold waters, which play a crucial role in global ocean circulation.

Arctic Ocean

he smallest and shallowest of the five oceans, it's located around the North Pole, mostly within the Arctic Circle.

Despite its size, it plays a significant role in global climate, especially as concerns grow about melting ice in the region due to global warming.
